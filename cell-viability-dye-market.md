<p><ul><li>DNA Binding Dye</li><li>Amine Reactive Dye</li><li>Enzyme Activated Dye</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Cell Viability Dye market features three main types: DNA Binding Dyes, Amine Reactive Dyes, and Enzyme Activated Dyes. DNA Binding Dyes selectively attach to nucleic acids, indicating cell viability through fluorescence. Amine Reactive Dyes target amino groups on proteins, signaling live or dead cells based on their reactivity. Enzyme Activated Dyes are designed to become fluorescent only when specific cellular enzymes are active, providing insight into metabolic activity. Together, these dyes facilitate diverse applications in research and diagnostics.</p></p>
