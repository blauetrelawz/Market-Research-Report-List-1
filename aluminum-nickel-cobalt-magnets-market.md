<p><p>The Aluminum-Nickel-Cobalt (Alnico) magnets market is primarily divided into two types: cast type and sintered type. Cast type Alnico magnets are produced by melting the alloy and allowing it to solidify in a mold, resulting in complex shapes with uniform magnetic properties. In contrast, sintered type magnets are created by compacting the powder of the alloy and then sintering it at high temperatures, which results in higher density and strength. Both types are used in various industrial applications, offering unique performance characteristics.</p></p>
