<p><ul><li>LEO</li><li>GEO</li><li>MEO</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Satellite markets are categorized based on their orbits: Low Earth Orbit (LEO), Geostationary Orbit (GEO), Medium Earth Orbit (MEO), and others. LEO satellites, positioned around 180 to 2,000 km above Earth, provide low-latency data for applications like remote sensing and communication. GEO satellites, stationed approximately 35,786 km above the equator, offer consistent coverage and are ideal for broadcasting and weather monitoring. MEO satellites, at altitudes between LEO and GEO, support navigation systems like GPS. Other markets include specialized satellites for unique missions or emerging technologies.</p></p>
