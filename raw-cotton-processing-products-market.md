<p><p>The raw cotton processing market consists of three main products: cotton fiber, cotton linters, and cottonseed. Cotton fiber, the primary product, is used for textiles and clothing, prized for its softness and breathability. Cotton linters, short fibers left after ginning, are utilized in products like paper, insulation, and cotton balls. Cottonseed, a byproduct, is processed for oil extraction, animal feed, and various food products. Together, these products highlight the versatility and economic significance of raw cotton processing.</p></p>
