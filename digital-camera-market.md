<p><p>The digital camera market is typically divided into two main types: built-in lens cameras, commonly known as point-and-shoot cameras, and interchangeable lens cameras, which include DSLRs and mirrorless interchangeable lens cameras (MILCs). Point-and-shoot cameras are user-friendly, compact, and designed for casual photography with fixed lenses. In contrast, DSLRs and MILCs offer greater flexibility and advanced features, allowing users to swap lenses for various shooting conditions, making them suitable for enthusiasts and professionals seeking higher image quality and creative control.</p></p>
